Forex market basics

  • What is Forex, and how does it work?

    The Forex (foreign exchange) market is the global currency exchange network. Trading involves buying one currency and selling another to profit from price differences. Major pairs include EUR/USD, GBP/JPY, and others.

  • What risks are involved in Forex trading?

    Forex is highly volatile, with prices fluctuating in real time. Key risks include exchange rate fluctuations, economic events, and leverage, which can amplify both gains and losses.

  • How to choose currency pairs for trading?

    Market newcomers should start with major pairs like EUR/USD or GBP/USD, as they are less volatile and have more predictable trends. Your choice should align with your trading strategy and market conditions.

  • What is leverage, and how does it work?

    Leverage allows traders to open larger positions with a smaller deposit. For example, with 1:100 leverage, you control $10,000 with just $100. While it increases profit potential, it also raises risks.

  • How to start Forex trading with minimal risk?

    To reduce risks, start with small positions, use stop-loss orders, study market trends, and stay updated with news. Develop a solid strategy and stick to it.
